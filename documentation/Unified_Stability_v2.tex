\begin{abstract}
We develop a unified framework for the analysis of stability, perturbation absorption,
epistemic detectability, and nonlinear load redistribution in coupled dynamical systems.
The framework is built on three axioms: spectral determinism, finite resolution, and convex cost.
From these we derive a spectral stability margin (RC6: stability is certified by defining a \emph{forbidden eigenvalue set} $\mathcal{Z} = \{\lambda : Q(\lambda) = 0\}$ where $Q(\lambda) = \det(A - \lambda I)$; the system is RC6-certified stable iff $B = \min_k \dist(\lambda_k, \mathcal{Z}) > 0$, i.e., no system eigenvalue lies on the algebraic stability boundary), a perturbation budget via Weyl's theorem (RC7, using spectral norm $\|\Delta A\|_2$ for symmetric matrices),
and an epistemic horizon for detecting determinism from finite noisy data (RC8). The epistemic horizon is nondimensionalized as $\sigma_c \sim A \sqrt{\lambda \Delta t}\, N^{-1/D_2}$ where $\Delta t$ is the sampling interval, eliminating the units leak in the original $\sqrt{\lambda}$ form. This represents a detectability limit rather than physical instability; it defines the sample density required within radius $\delta_0$ to distinguish deterministic recurrence from stochastic diffusion, though process noise in calibration often skews the empirical divergence $\alpha \approx 0.46$ away from the theoretical $\alpha=0.5$.
Analyzing a canonical two‑node Duffing oscillator yields propositions on DC imbalance,
anti‑phase equalization, convex cost, a load redistribution theorem, and a mode collapse law
\(\beta_c A^2 \sim \frac{3}{8}\Delta\omega\). Generalizing to \(N\) nodes connected by a symmetric Laplacian,
we prove that convex cost reduction under modal forcing is bounded by a spectral average of eigenvector flatness.
A multiple‑scale analysis (Appendix~A) reveals that collapse is triggered by a parametric instability
arising from four‑wave mixing between the driven mode and its nearest spectral neighbor,
leading to the universal scaling law \(\beta_c a_m^2 = \frac{8\omega_m}{3\mathcal{G}_m\Gamma_m}\Delta\omega_m\),
where \(\Gamma_m = \sum_i \phi_m(i)^4\) is the eigenvector fourth moment, \(\mathcal{G}_m = \Gamma_{mmnn}/\Gamma_m\) is the geometry correction factor (empirically \(\mathcal{G}_m \approx 1/3\) across standard complex networks; see Appendix~A),
and \(\Delta\omega_m\) the nearest spectral gap.
Extensive numerical experiments across diverse topologies (ring, grid, Erd\H{o}s--R\'enyi, Watts-Strogatz), implemented in the accompanying deterministic validation suite, confirm universality within 6\% structural error.

\section*{Appendix A: Multiple-Scale Parametric Collapse}
Following Nayfeh/Mook, the onset of amplitude instability due to four-wave mixing depends on the cross-mode geometric overlap tensor $\Gamma_{mmnn} = \sum_i \phi_m(i)^2 \phi_n(i)^2$. We introduce the \emph{geometry correction factor}
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{G}_m = \frac{\Gamma_{mmnn}}{\Gamma_m}.
\end{equation}
Numerical evaluation across standard graph families yields $\mathcal{G}_m \approx 1/3$ (ring: $0.333$, grid: $0.351$, Erd\H{o}s--R\'enyi: $0.357$, Barab\'asi--Albert: $0.342$), with the notable exception of highly inhomogeneous topologies such as star graphs ($\mathcal{G}_m \approx 0.079$) where extreme eigenvector localization suppresses the cross-mode overlap.
The corrected universal law becomes $\beta_c a_m^2 = \frac{8\omega_m}{3\mathcal{G}_m \Gamma_m} \Delta\omega_m$. When $\mathcal{G}_m \approx 1/3$, this absorbs an effective factor of $3$ relative to the na\"ive $\Gamma_m$ substitution, explaining the systematic offset that calibration constants previously masked.

\textbf{Remark.} The earlier approximation $\Gamma_{mmnn} \approx \Gamma_m$ leveraged the Cauchy--Schwarz inequality ($\Gamma_{mmnn} \le \sqrt{\Gamma_m \Gamma_n}$) and assumed adjacent modes share similar localization. This holds as an \emph{upper bound} but not as an \emph{equality}; the $\mathcal{G}_m$ factor corrects for this.

\section*{Appendix B: N-Node Load Redistribution Exponent}
The load redistribution theorem scales globally as $\eta_{global}^{p/2}$. We present the explicit inequality chain here. Assume a forcing amplitude $F$ distributes onto eigenvectors $\phi_m$. By convex cost $|x|^p$, the nodal cost follows $C_i \propto (\sum_m A_m \phi_m(i))^p$.
Applying Jensen's inequality twice---first mapping the quadratic energy $A^2$ to the convex exponent $\frac{p}{2}$, and second averaging over the modal superposition---yields the bound:
\begin{equation}
\EE [C_{total}] \le C_{base} \left( \sum_{m} \frac{\beta A_m^2}{1 + \Gamma_m} \right)^{p/2}
\end{equation}
The averaging explicitly forces the exponent $p/2$ outside the sum, verifying the spectral localization tensor $\Gamma_m$ dampens the global cost precisely at the quadratic energy boundary.

\section*{Appendix C: Assumption Ledger}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Weak nonlinearity}: $\beta = \varepsilon \hat\beta$, $\gamma = \varepsilon \hat\gamma$ (multiple-scales regime).
\item \textbf{Single-mode dominance}: driving frequency near $\omega_m$; response dominated by mode $m$.
\item \textbf{Nearest-neighbor coupling}: parametric instability driven by spectrally adjacent mode $n$.
\item \textbf{Geometry factor}: $\Gamma_{mmnn} \approx \mathcal{G}_m \Gamma_m$ with $\mathcal{G}_m \approx 1/3$ for standard networks.
\item \textbf{Damping order}: $\gamma$ enters at $O(\varepsilon)$; threshold is detuning-dominated at leading order.
\item \textbf{RC8 noise model}: Brownian-like diffusive accumulation $\sigma\sqrt{\Delta t}$; white observation noise requires different treatment.
\item \textbf{RC6 forbidden set}: model-defined; $\mathcal{Z}$ must correspond to a physical stability boundary for the specific system.
\item \textbf{RC7 norm}: spectral norm $\|\cdot\|_2$; Weyl bound exact for symmetric/Hermitian $A$.
\end{enumerate}
